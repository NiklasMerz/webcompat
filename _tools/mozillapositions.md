## What it does

A GitHub repository where Mozilla records its formal position on web standards proposals — specifications, APIs, and features being developed at W3C, WHATWG, TC39, and other bodies. Each position entry is filed as a GitHub issue and given one of a small set of labels: "positive", "neutral", "negative", or "harmful". The rationale for each position is discussed openly in the issue thread.

## Who is it for

Standards editors and proposal authors seeking early signal from Mozilla, web developers trying to understand Mozilla's implementation intentions, and anyone tracking multi-vendor consensus around a proposed web feature.

## Where to find it

[github.com/mozilla/standards-positions](https://github.com/mozilla/standards-positions)

## Who is maintaining it

Mozilla's standards team. Positions are submitted and reviewed by Mozilla engineers and product managers with domain expertise in the relevant area.
