## What it does

Defines a clear threshold for when a web feature is safe to use: "Baseline Newly Available" means it has shipped in all major browsers within the last 30 months; "Baseline Widely Available" means it has been available for over 30 months. Removes guesswork from the "can I use this?" question.

## Who is it for

Web developers who want a simple signal for feature readiness without having to interpret raw compat tables. Also useful for educators, bloggers, and documentation authors characterising feature maturity.

## Where to find it

[https://web-platform-dx.github.io/web-features/](https://web-platform-dx.github.io/web-features/) for more information. Baseline status is surfaced on MDN, web.dev, and CanIUse and more.

## Who is maintaining it

Originally driven by Google Chrome, now defined collaboratively through the WebDX Community Group with input from Mozilla, Apple, and Microsoft.
