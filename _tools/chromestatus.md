## What it does

Tracks the implementation status of web platform features in Chrome, along with Google's formal position on proposed standards. Each feature entry shows its current development stage (from "Proposed" through "Shipped"), links to the relevant specification and Chromium bug, and records intents posted to blink-dev — the mailing list where Chromium engineers seek consensus before implementing or shipping a feature. Also publishes a timeline of what shipped in each Chrome release.

## Who is it for

Web developers checking when a feature will land in Chrome or what Chrome's current stance is, standards contributors following implementation signals across engines, and web compat researchers tracking new capabilities and their rollout.

## Where to find it

[chromestatus.com](https://chromestatus.com)

## Who is maintaining it

Google, as part of the Chromium project. The source is open at [github.com/GoogleChrome/chromium-dashboard](https://github.com/GoogleChrome/chromium-dashboard).
